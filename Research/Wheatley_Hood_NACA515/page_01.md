# REPORT No. 515

## FULL-SCALE WIND-TUNNEL TESTS OF A PCA-2 AUTOGIRO ROTOR

By JOHN B. WHEATLEY and MICHEL B. HOOD

### SUMMARY

An investigation has been made in the full-scale wind tunnel of the aerodynamic characteristics of a PCA-2 autogiro rotor. The forces and moments on the rotor were measured for a range of angles of attack and tip-speed ratios. In addition, the flapping and feathering motions of the blades were recorded. The tests were made with and without fairings on the blade-root supporting members.

The results show that the maximum lift-drag ratio of the rotor was 7.1. The maximum value of the ratio of the thrust to the torque was 6.5. The maximum lift coefficient obtained was 0.68, which occurred at a tip-speed ratio of 0.145. The lift coefficient was found to be independent of the rotational speed.

The drag of the protuberances at the blade roots was found to be large, amounting to about 30 percent of the minimum drag of the complete autogiro. The effect of the protuberances on the lift was negligible.

The analysis of the blade motion shows that the flapping of the blades can be predicted with good accuracy by the existing theory. The feathering motion, which is the periodic change in pitch of the blades, was found to be small.

### INTRODUCTION

The autogiro has been developed to a practical stage by Cierva and his associates without the aid of extensive wind-tunnel research. The flight-test method of development has been used almost exclusively. While this method has been successful in producing a practical aircraft, it has not provided the detailed information on the aerodynamic characteristics of the rotor that is necessary for a complete understanding of the subject. The present investigation was undertaken to provide some of this information.

The tests were made in the full-scale wind tunnel of the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics. The object of the tests was to determine the forces, moments, and blade motion of a PCA-2 autogiro rotor over a wide range of operating conditions. The rotor was tested with and without fairings on the blade-root supporting members to determine the effect of these protuberances.
