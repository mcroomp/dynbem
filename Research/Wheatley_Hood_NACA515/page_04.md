# REPORT No. 515 - PCA-2 AUTOGIRO ROTOR (Page 4)

**Blade-Motion Tests**

The flapping motion of the blades is shown in figures 14 and 15. The flapping angle, `β`, is plotted against the azimuth angle, `ψ`, for several tip-speed ratios. The azimuth angle is measured from the downwind position in the direction of rotation. The flapping angle is the angle between the blade and the plane of the hub.

The curves in figure 14 show that the flapping motion is approximately sinusoidal. The amplitude of the flapping increases as the tip-speed ratio increases. The maximum flapping angle was about 8°, which occurred at a tip-speed ratio of 0.5.

The phase angle of the flapping motion is shown in figure 15. The phase angle is the azimuth angle at which the blade reaches its maximum upward flapping position. The theoretical phase angle is 90° behind the position of maximum lift. The experimental results show that the phase angle is about 100° for low tip-speed ratios and increases to about 120° for high tip-speed ratios.

A comparison of the experimental flapping motion with the theory of reference 1 is shown in figure 16. The theoretical flapping angle is given by the equation:

`β = a_0 - a_1 * cos(ψ) - b_1 * sin(ψ)`

where `a_0` is the coning angle, and `a_1` and `b_1` are the longitudinal and lateral flapping coefficients. The coefficients are functions of the tip-speed ratio, the lift coefficient, and the blade characteristics. The theoretical curves in figure 16 were computed for the conditions of the test. The agreement between the theory and the experiment is good.

The feathering motion of the blades is shown in figure 17. The feathering angle is the periodic change in the pitch of the blades due to the flexibility of the structure. The feathering motion was found to be small, the maximum amplitude being about 0.5°. The motion is approximately sinusoidal and is 180° out of phase with the flapping motion.

### CONCLUSIONS

1. The maximum lift-drag ratio of the PCA-2 rotor was 7.1.
2. The maximum lift coefficient was 0.68, which occurred at a tip-speed ratio of 0.145.
3. The lift coefficient was independent of the rotational speed for the range of the tests.
4. The drag of the protuberances at the blade roots was about 30 percent of the minimum drag of the complete autogiro.
5. The flapping motion of the blades can be predicted with good accuracy by the existing theory.
6. The feathering motion of the blades was small.
