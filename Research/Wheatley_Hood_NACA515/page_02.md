# REPORT No. 515 - PCA-2 AUTOGIRO ROTOR

### APPARATUS AND METHODS

The rotor was mounted in the wind tunnel on a special test rig, as shown in figure 1. The rig consisted of a welded tubular-steel framework that supported the rotor hub through a vertical torque tube. The torque tube was mounted in ball bearings and was connected to a hydraulic torque-measuring unit. The entire rig was supported on the main balance of the wind tunnel, so that the lift and the drag of the rotor could be measured. The angle of attack of the rotor was varied by tilting the complete rig about a transverse axis.

The rotor hub was of the standard PCA-2 type. It provided for the attachment of the four blades through double hinges. The horizontal hinges permitted the blades to flap, and the vertical hinges permitted them to move in the plane of rotation. The flapping hinges were located 13.5 inches from the center of rotation. The drag hinges were 21.5 inches from the center. The flapping motion was limited by rubber-in-compression bumpers, and the drag motion was damped by friction dampers. The blades were connected to the hub by flexible steel straps, which transmitted the centrifugal force.

The blades were of conventional construction, with a steel-tube spar and wood ribs covered with fabric. The airfoil section was a Göttingen 429. The blades had a uniform chord of 20 inches and a length of 18 feet 6 inches from the center of the flapping hinge. The total radius of the rotor was 22 feet. The blades were set at a pitch angle of 2.7° at the root. There was no twist. The solidity of the rotor was 0.043.

The rotational speed of the rotor was controlled by a prony brake mounted on the torque tube. The brake was operated from the control room of the wind tunnel. The rotational speed was measured by a chronometric tachometer.

The flapping and feathering motions of the blades were measured by means of a special camera mounted on the rotor hub. The camera recorded the position of a light on the blade tip at various points in the revolution. The light was a small incandescent lamp mounted in a streamlined housing. The power was supplied to the lamp through slip rings. The camera was driven by a small electric motor. The exposure was controlled by a shutter that was synchronized with the rotation of the rotor.

The tests were made at a tunnel air speed of approximately 100 feet per second. The corresponding Reynolds Number, based on the blade chord and the tip speed in hovering, was about 1,000,000. The forces and moments were measured for a range of tip-speed ratios from 0.1 to 0.55 and for a range of angles of attack from 0° to 20°. The tip-speed ratio is defined as the ratio of the forward velocity of the aircraft to the rotational tip speed of the blades.

The coefficients used to present the results are defined as follows:

`C_L = L / (ρ * A * (ΩR)^2)`
`C_D = D / (ρ * A * (ΩR)^2)`
`C_M = M / (ρ * A * (ΩR)^2 * R)`
`C_T = T / (ρ * A * (ΩR)^2)`
`C_Q = Q / (ρ * A * (ΩR)^2 * R)`

where:
- `L` is the lift, the component of the resultant force perpendicular to the flight path.
- `D` is the drag, the component of the resultant force parallel to the flight path.
- `M` is the pitching moment about the hub center.
- `T` is the thrust, the component of the resultant force along the axis of rotation.
- `Q` is the torque about the axis of rotation.
- `A` is the disk area, `πR^2`.
- `R` is the rotor radius.
- `Ω` is the angular velocity of the rotor.
- `ρ` is the mass density of the air.

The angle of attack, `α`, is the angle between the flight path and the plane of the hub. The tip-speed ratio, `μ`, is `V / (ΩR)`, where `V` is the forward velocity.

The forces and moments were measured about the wind axes. The lift and the drag were then converted to thrust and torque by the following relations:

`T = L * cos(α) + D * sin(α)`
`H = D * cos(α) - L * sin(α)`

where `H` is the force in the plane of rotation. The torque is measured directly. The pitching moment is given about the hub center. The rolling and yawing moments were not measured.
