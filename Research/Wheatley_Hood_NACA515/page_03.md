# REPORT No. 515 - PCA-2 AUTOGIRO ROTOR (Page 3)

### PRECISION

The forces and moments were measured with the standard balance equipment of the wind tunnel. The precision of the measurements is indicated by the following table:

| Quantity | Precision |
| :--- | :--- |
| Lift, L | ±2 pounds |
| Drag, D | ±1 pound |
| Pitching moment, M | ±5 pound-feet |
| Torque, Q | ±2 pound-feet |
| Angle of attack, α | ±0.1° |
| Air speed, V | ±0.2 feet per second |
| Rotational speed, N | ±0.5 rpm |

The flapping and feathering angles were measured from the photographic records to within ±0.1°.

### RESULTS AND DISCUSSION

The results of the tests are presented in the form of curves showing the variation of the force and moment coefficients with the tip-speed ratio for several angles of attack. The data are given for the rotor with and without the blade-root fairings. The blade-motion data are presented in the form of curves showing the variation of the flapping and feathering angles with the azimuth position of the blade.

**Force and Moment Tests**

The variation of the lift coefficient with the angle of attack for several tip-speed ratios is shown in figure 2. It will be seen that the lift coefficient increases with the angle of attack up to a maximum value and then decreases. The angle of attack for maximum lift increases as the tip-speed ratio decreases. The maximum lift coefficient obtained was 0.68, which occurred at a tip-speed ratio of 0.145 and an angle of attack of 17°.

The effect of the tip-speed ratio on the lift coefficient for several angles of attack is shown in figure 3. For a constant angle of attack, the lift coefficient increases as the tip-speed ratio decreases until the stalling angle is reached.

The variation of the drag coefficient with the lift coefficient is shown in figure 4. The drag coefficient includes the profile drag of the blades and the induced drag of the rotor. The minimum drag coefficient was about 0.008.

The lift-drag ratio, L/D, is plotted against the lift coefficient in figure 5. The maximum L/D was 7.1, which occurred at a lift coefficient of 0.4.

The variation of the torque coefficient with the lift coefficient is shown in figure 6. The torque coefficient is a measure of the power required to drive the rotor. The torque coefficient increases with the lift coefficient. The minimum torque coefficient was about 0.0001.

The ratio of the thrust to the torque, T/Q, is a measure of the efficiency of the rotor as a lifting device. This ratio is plotted against the thrust coefficient in figure 7. The maximum value of T/Q was 6.5, which occurred at a thrust coefficient of 0.45.

The effect of the blade-root protuberances on the forces and moments is shown in figures 8 to 11. The protuberances consisted of the blade-root fittings and the flapping and drag hinges. The drag of these parts was found to be large. Figure 8 shows that the drag of the rotor with the protuberances exposed was about 30 percent greater than the drag with the protuberances faired. The effect of the protuberances on the lift was negligible, as shown in figure 9. The effect on the L/D and the T/Q is shown in figures 10 and 11. The maximum L/D was reduced from 7.1 to 5.8 by the drag of the protuberances. The maximum T/Q was reduced from 6.5 to 5.0.

The effect of rotational speed on the lift coefficient is shown in figure 12. The data were obtained at four rotational speeds, from 98 to 148 r.p.m. The lift coefficient is seen to be independent of the rotational speed within this range. This result indicates that the scale effect on the lift is negligible for the range of Reynolds Number covered in the tests.

The pitching-moment characteristics of the rotor are shown in figure 13. The pitching-moment coefficient is plotted against the tip-speed ratio for several angles of attack. The pitching moment is the moment about the hub center and is positive when it tends to raise the nose of the aircraft. The results show that the rotor has a diving moment at low tip-speed ratios and a stalling moment at high tip-speed ratios. The moment is small for the normal range of operating tip-speed ratios.
